# Liquidity

**Liquidity** = how easily you can trade an asset *quickly*, in *size*, without *moving the price much*. A liquid market has lots of resting orders close to the current price. An illiquid market is thin — a modest order jumps the price.

Three dimensions:
- **Tightness** — how small the [[Bid-Ask-Spread]] is.
- **Depth** — how much volume is resting at each price level (Milestone 4: market depth).
- **Resiliency** — how fast the book refills after a trade eats through it.

Provided by [[Market-Maker]]s (passive orders), consumed by takers (aggressive orders). See [[Passive-vs-Aggressive]].
